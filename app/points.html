Points apart

Discharge point · monitoring point · WINEP action — linked by identifier, scattered on the ground
Docs SPARQL ← Back to app

A permit's discharge point, the monitoring point it is sampled at, and any WINEP action proposed for it all describe the same regulated works — yet in the source data they sit hundreds of metres to over a kilometre apart. All three are published by EA sources in the same coordinate reference system (British National Grid), so this is no projection artefact: they are genuinely different points on the ground. Linked data joins them deterministically by identifier (monitoredAt, targetPermit); a GIS "join what's nearby" cannot, without either missing them or sweeping in unrelated neighbours.

The opposite failure is just as bad. Outlets that are different things often share one coordinate. At Brockhill Watercress Farm, seven outlets belonging to four permits (043245, 043244, 401057, 401058) sit on a single point — but the EA samples them at four different points, 120–265 m apart.

A nearest-point join has to give all seven outlets the same answer, so it gets one of them right. monitoredAt gets all seven right.
